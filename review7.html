Key Differences: Comparing Cell Types

🦓

Animal Cells

Eukaryotic cells that make up animal tissues. Highly flexible and compartmentalized.

  • ✓Contain Lysosomes for waste removal.
  • ✓Contain Centrosomes for cell division.
  • ✗Lack a rigid Cell Wall.
🌿

Plant Cells

Eukaryotic cells engineered for structural stability and autotrophic energy production.

  • ✓Contain a rigid Cell Wall (cellulose).
  • ✓Contain Chloroplasts for photosynthesis.
  • ✓Feature a large Central Vacuole.
🦠

Prokaryotic Cells

Structurally simpler, evolutionarily older cells, characteristic of Bacteria.

  • ✗Lack a true nucleus.
  • ✗Lack membrane-bound organelles.
  • ✓Store DNA in an unbound nucleoid region.

The Cell Cycle & Regulation

The life of a cell is meticulously timed and regulated by cyclins and CDKs. The vast majority of a cell's existence is spent in Interphase preparing for the dramatic event of division.

G1 Phase (First Gap)

Cell growth and normal metabolic roles. The G1 Checkpoint ensures conditions are favorable and DNA is undamaged.

S Phase (Synthesis)

DNA replication occurs. Every single chromosome is duplicated to form two identical sister chromatids.

G2 Phase (Second Gap)

Final growth and preparation for division. The G2 Checkpoint confirms DNA replication was completely successful and error-free.

M Phase (Mitotic Phase)

Mitosis (nuclear division) and Cytokinesis. The M Checkpoint ensures all chromosomes are properly attached to spindle fibers.

Approximate time spent in each phase (standard 24h cycle).

Division Mechanics

Cells divide via two primary processes. Mitosis ensures exact somatic cloning for growth and repair, while Meiosis generates unique haploid gametes for sexual reproduction.

Division Outcome Comparison

Mitosis Outcome

1 parent cell divides once to produce 2 genetically identical, diploid (2n) somatic cells.

Meiosis Outcome

1 parent cell divides twice to produce 4 genetically distinct, haploid (n) germ cells.
