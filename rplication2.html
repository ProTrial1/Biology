📜 Foundations: Models of Replication

DNA replication is the process by which DNA makes a copy of itself. The double helix consists of two anti-parallel strands bound by the AT/GC rule. Historically, three models were proposed for how this duplication occurs. The Semiconservative Model was proven correct.

⚙ The Replisome Machinery

DNA synthesis is powered by a highly coordinated assembly of proteins called the replisome. Because DNA polymerases can only attach nucleotides in the 5' to 3' direction, the two strands (Leading and Lagging) are synthesized differently. Select an enzyme below to view its specific function.

➡ Leading Strand

Synthesized continuously in the same direction that the replication fork is opening. Requires only a single initial RNA primer.

⬅ Lagging Strand

Synthesized discontinuously away from the fork. Built in short segments called Okazaki Fragments, each requiring its own RNA primer.

Bacterial vs. Eukaryotic Systems

While the fundamental chemistry is identical, the scale and complexity of linear eukaryotic chromosomes necessitate specialized mechanisms compared to circular bacterial DNA.

Bacterial Replication

🦠
  • ✓
    Single Origin (oriC) Contains AT-rich region, DnaA boxes, and GATC methylation sites. Unwound by DnaA proteins.
  • ✓
    Regulation by Methylation Dam methylase methylates GATC sites. Replication initiates only when fully methylated, coordinating with cell division.
  • ✓
    Termination Forks meet at opposite sides of the circle at ter sequences, stopped by Tus proteins. Interlocked circles (catenanes) are decatenated.

Eukaryotic Replication

🧬
  • ✓
    Multiple Origins Linear chromosomes use many origins (ARS elements in yeast) recognized by the Origin Recognition Complex (ORC).
  • ✓
    The Polymerase Switch Pol α (alpha) acts with primase, then swaps with Pol δ (delta) or ε (epsilon) for processive elongation.
  • ✓
    Nucleosome Assembly Newly synthesized DNA is rapidly wrapped around a random mixture of old and newly synthesized histone octamers.

⚠ The Telomere Problem

Because DNA polymerase requires a primer and works 5' to 3', the extreme 3' ends of linear eukaryotic chromosomes cannot be fully replicated. Without a mechanism to fix this, chromosomes would shorten with each cell division.

The Telomerase Solution

Telomerase is a unique enzyme that contains its own RNA template. It binds to the 3' overhang of telomeres (repetitive sequences) and synthesizes additional DNA repeats. Once extended, standard primase and polymerase can fill in the complementary bottom strand.
