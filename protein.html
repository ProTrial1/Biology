📖 The Blueprint of Life

Protein synthesis is the biological process by which individual cells build their specific proteins. Within the process are involved both DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) and different in their function RNAs (ribonucleic acids). The process is initiated in the cell's nucleus, where specific enzymes unwind the needed section of DNA, which makes the DNA in this region accessible and a RNA copy can be made.

1. The Master Code

DNA resides safely in the nucleus, holding the long-term master instructions for every protein the organism will ever need.

2. The Messenger

Messenger RNA (mRNA) acts as a temporary copy of a specific gene, carrying the code out of the nucleus to the cell's factories.

3. The Factory

Ribosomes (made of rRNA) read the mRNA and link together Amino Acids (brought by tRNA) to form the final protein chain.
